Valakran I: Dead World

Mineral-rich, supports no life. Occasional visits from miners.

CONTROLLED BY: Raiders.

CONTROL EFFECT: Once per game, at the beginning of your Command phase, you may restore D3 wounds to a "Vehicle" or "Monster" unit. This die result cannot be re-rolled.

CONTROL FLAVOR: Valakran I is littered with abandoned refineries, partially-refined metals, and scrap minerals. These can be used for emergency battlefield repairs, or to reinforce chitinous carapaces.

Valakran II: Paradise World

Popular destination with Imperial nobility in Segmentum Ultima.

CONTROLLED BY: Invaders

CONTROL EFFECT:

  • Defenders: Once per game, you may use the Insane Bravery strategem for 0 CP.
  • Invaders: Once per game, you may use the Epic Challenge strategem for 0 CP.
  • Raiders: Once per game, you may force your opponent to re-roll a Battle Shock test, subtracting 2 from the result.

CONTROL FLAVOR: Control of this vacation destination either gives the citizens hope and raises the morale of your forces... or weakens citizens resolve and redoubles your force's efforts of extermination.

Valakran III: Hive World

Dominated by two Hive Cities, hosts Psyker training facilities.

See planetside for control bonuses.

Valakran IV: Dead World

Covered in frozen nitrogen, unsuitable for Imperial use.

CONTROLLED BY: None.

CONTROL EFFECT: None.

CONTROL FLAVOR: Valakran IV is devoid of any resources or lives worth using.

Valakran V: Forge World

Long ago, a failed terraforming project. Now, one massive Hive City supporting a Manufactorum.

See planetside for control bonuses.

Valakran VI: Dead World

Stripped of resources, littered with abandoned mining sites.

CONTROLLED BY: Invaders

CONTROL EFFECT: You may automatically succeed an Out Of Action test for one unit after a battle.

CONTROL FLAVOR: Valakran VI, due to its lack of biomass, offers the safest position in the Valakran system to regroup between engagements.

Valakran VII: Death World

Inhabited by large monsters with rock-like hides. Planet occasionally causes Warp interference for astropaths. Source of interference has never been identified.

CONTROLLED BY: Raiders

CONTROL EFFECT: The first time your opponent would use the Command Re-roll strategem, it costs an additional 1 CP.

CONTROL FLAVOR: While no one can identify the source, you find that the strange Warp interference can be focused and re-directed.
